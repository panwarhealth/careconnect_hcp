Why is this activity important for GPs?

GPs are often the first point of contact for patients who experience anorectal conditions such as anal fissures.1,2 However, the associated stigma means that patients may find it difficult to discuss their concerns, which may lead to underdiagnosis and inadequate treatment.2,3

 

What is involved in this activity?

This two-part educational activity is designed for GPs to improve their assessment and identification of anal fissures and support best-practice management for patients with this condition.

  • Online Learning Module: covers key information on pathophysiology and presentations of anal fissures, as well as protocols for to guide you through screening, assessment, management, and patient counselling.

  • Mini Clinical Audit: provides a framework for you to review your historical clinical practices and implement improved management of current and future patients with anal fissures.
